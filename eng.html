About the Monastery
The monastery is located in Israel near the ancient city of Capernaum on the northwest coast of the Sea of Tiberias (now the Sea of Galilee) in Galilee, Israel. Jesus Christ preached in the synagogue of Capernaum and performed many miracles in this city. The Cathedral of St. Luke, consecrated in 1987, is located on the monastery grounds. There are also male dormitories for pilgrims.
The Divine Liturgy of the venerable Alexy - Russian spiritual mission is performed daily.
The monastery always observes winter time. Therefore, services start at different times in the summer and winter periods. Festive and celebratory services are performed in the Cathedral of All Saints who Shone in the Russian Land. Ordinary services are held in the Temple of St. Luke at different times.

About Reverend Alexy
Saint Alexis was born in Rome into a family of pious ungodly-loving Euthymian and Aglaida. The couple have been for a long time childless and tirelessly prayed to the Lord for the gift of offspring. AND The Lord consoled the couple with the birth of their son Alexy. At six years old the lad began to study and successfully studied secular sciences, but especially diligently read the Holy Scriptures. As a young man, he began imitate their parents: strictly fasted, distributed alms and under rich clothes he secretly wore a sackcloth. It ripened early desire to leave the world and serve the One God. However, parents were going to marry Alexy and, when he came of age, found him a bride.
After the betrothal, left alone with his bride in the evening, Alexy removed the ring from his finger, gave it to her and said: "Keep this, and may the Lord be with us, by His grace arranging for us new life." And he himself secretly left home and boarded a ship, sailed to Mesopotamia.
Once in the city of Edessa, where the image of the Lord not made by hands was kept, Alexy sold everything he had, distributed money to the poor and became live at the Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary on the porch and feed alms. The reverend ate only bread and water, and He distributed the alms he received to the weak and the elderly. Each On Sunday he received the Holy Mysteries.
Relatives searched everywhere for the missing Alexy, but to no avail. Servants, sent by Euthymian to search, they also visited Edessa, but did not recognized in the beggar, sitting on the porch, their master. From strict fasting, his body dried up, beauty disappeared, vision became weak. The blessed one recognized them and thanked the Lord for received alms from his servants.
The body of the saint from whom the healings began was placed in the middle of the square. All Rome gathered here. Emperor and The Pope themselves brought the body of the saint into the church where it was for a whole week, and then was laid in a marble tomb. From holy relics began to flow fragrant myrrh, giving healing for the sick.
The honest remains of St. Alexis, the man of God, are buried in Church of Saint Boniface. In 1216 relics were found.
The life of the holy Reverend Alexis, a man of God, has always been one of the favorites in Rus'.
